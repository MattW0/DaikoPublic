## Intro 
Darkness. Not the comforting kind that comes with sleep — this one thinks. It presses against you, whispering in voices that sound like your own. You feel weightless. Drifting. Somewhere far away, metal rattles. Chains, perhaps. Or the hum of a machine. Then, the Key — that cold, alien weight in your mind — pulses once, twice… like a heartbeat that isn’t yours.

And with each pulse, memories flicker before you. Moments of your life flash like reflections in black water — laughter, fear, blood, friendship. Then they start to peel away, pulled gently from you, thread by thread, drawn into a box of impossible design — a box that seems to exist behind your eyes, yet beyond the world.

You can feel it taking everything — not cruelly, but with purpose. Copying you. Archiving every thought, every scar, every secret. And for a heartbeat, you’re certain there’s another you forming in that darkness, somewhere just out of sight.

Then — noise. The rhythmic grind of wagon wheels on cobblestone. Voices, muffled by iron and distance. The air grows thicker, colder. You try to move — but your body is heavy. You try to speak — but your throat answers only with silence. The darkness fades slowly into a gray haze. Then — light.

Your eyes snap open. The smell hits you first: damp stone, rust, and sweat. You’re lying on a rough cot in a narrow cell. The walls are carved from thick blocks of black granite, cold to the touch.
Somewhere beyond the bars, you hear footsteps… the dull clang of metal… and the sound of someone crying, softly, from another cell.

You’re alone. Your gear — gone. Your head — pounding. And somewhere in the back of your mind, you can feel the key, still pulsing faintly. Almost as if it’s waiting for you to remember something you’ve already forgotten.
