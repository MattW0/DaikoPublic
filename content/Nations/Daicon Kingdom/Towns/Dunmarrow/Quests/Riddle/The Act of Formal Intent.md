Snail Folk courtship is inseparable from their devotion to civic order and public administration. Romantic intent is never expressed in private or informal spaces, but **within the public Gemeinde Verwaltung**, where names are recorded, documents issued, and identities officially recognized. Among the Snail Folk, affection must first pass through communal authority before it may become personal.

The most widely attested custom is the **Tri-Shell Offering**. An admirer is expected to go in person to the **public Gemeinde Verwaltung** and present **three polished snail shells**, unbroken and unremarkable, symbolizing patience, continuity, and lawful conduct. The shells are deliberately common and easily obtained, as sincerity is judged by correctness and adherence to procedure rather than rarity or expense. Tradition holds that the offering must be made **at the service counter**, in full view, during normal office hours.

The ritual requires the admirer to wait their turn, state their purpose calmly, and place the three shells forward without elaboration. Snail Folk belief maintains that those who work daily with registries, identification papers, and civic records are best suited to recognize genuine intent. If the shells are accepted and set aside rather than returned, the courtship is considered properly initiated.
