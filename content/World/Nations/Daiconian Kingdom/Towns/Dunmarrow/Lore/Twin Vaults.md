## **Initial Function (As Discovered by Players):**
The boxes function as a **paired teleportation container system**. Any non-living object placed into Box A instantly appears inside Box B, and vice versa. Distance is irrelevant. This effect works even across planar boundaries, provided both boxes are in the same plane of existence for at least one heartbeat.
### **Basic Observed Properties:**
>- Instantaneous item transfer between the two.
>- No known limit to distance.
>- Object size limit: Anything that fits physically within the box.
>- Box B has no visible hinges, yet opens simultaneously when Box A is opened (and vice versa).
>- Magical auras: Abjuration, Conjuration, and faint Divination.
## **Known Uses in Your Campaign:**
>- **Box A** is kept as a “register” in the player’s bar for daily earnings and orders.
>- **Box B** is carried by the party during adventures. This setup allows passive income transfer and magical communication of funds, notes, and materials.
## **Deeper Origin and Lore (Obscured):**
The Twin Vaults were once known as the **Chambers of Passage**, used by the _Custodians of the Cycle_, an order lost to myth. Created in the **Age of Echoed Light**, the boxes were said to be a fragment of an even greater artifact: _The Continuum Engine_—a divine mechanism meant to preserve balance across planar ley-points.
Their sigils predate mortal script. Most scholars misidentify them as stylized sun or storm runes, but in truth, they are part of a **non-Euclidean logic script** readable only by beings with perception of **Chrono-Planar Symmetry** (see Deciphering Steps).
## **True Properties (Locked):**
If fully deciphered, the Twin Vaults gain the following abilities:
### **Unbound Synchrony**
The vaults can teleport not only objects, but creatures, including unwilling targets.
Range: Same plane or interplanar (with anchor spell). Requires synchronized incantation by both attuned users.
### **Temporal Recovery**
Once per tenday, the user may reach into the Vault and retrieve a **lost item** that was once inside within the last 3 months.
Requires expenditure of 500 gp in arcane reagents and a successful DC 22 Arcana check.
### **Echo of the Custodians**
Upon full unlocking, each user hears fragments of a long-lost voice. These whispers may grant glimpses into past or future events (DM’s choice, 1/day).
May also begin attracting extraplanar attention.
## **Steps to Discovering the True Origin: (Optional Puzzle Trail)**
**Stage 1 – Hidden Mechanism (DC 20 Arcana or Detect Magic over a week):**
Uncover a shifting layer of runes hidden under the top carving. Only visible under moonlight or by casting _Comprehend Languages_ with three drops of blood placed on the lid.
**Stage 2 – Research (DC 25 History or Arcana, or discovery of an ancient manuscript):**
The phrase _“We carry not things, but burdens”_ must be spoken aloud while placing a token of personal memory into the box (a photo, a letter, etc.). This activates dormant magical glyphs and initiates a resonance.
**Stage 3 – Attunement Bond (Unique Ritual):**
Two creatures must **attune together**, speaking their true names over the open boxes while holding an item of the other’s past. If successful, the boxes glow and shift form slightly—revealing their second-tier abilities.
**Stage 4 – Revelation (DM-triggered):**
Contact from one of the surviving Custodians—or a hostile ancient being seeking the Continuum Engine—marks the boxes as **Legendary Artifacts**.
## **Cursed Effect (Optional Twist):**
Once fully awakened, the boxes become sentient in a slow, alien way. Occasionally, they deliver something **not placed** by the players: a withered hand, a faded letter signed in a language they don’t speak, a piece of gear lost years ago—or something that _shouldn’t exist_.
## **Final Notes:**
The boxes should appear as nothing more than rare magical containers until well into the campaign. The mystery of their function can evolve with the characters’ level, and fully unlocking their origin could even become a central arc—or a subtle subplot that unfolds in parallel to other major events.
### **How Jackie Came Across the Twin Vaults**
Jackie was playing a long con in **Port Thistle**, a coastal smuggler’s haven where contraband flows easier than wine. After a particularly _inspired_ evening (high on Crushed Obsidian Powder ), Jackie posed as a **broker of forgotten relics** and ended up winning a **sealed wooden crate** in a rigged dice game played in the backroom of a failing theater.
The crate was traded by a masked elf with a cough like broken glass, who said only:
> _“You win the Vault, bard. Best it stays unopened, but I wash my hands.”_
Inside were the two boxes—heavy, cold to the touch, and humming with a subtle tension. Jackie, assuming it was smuggler tech or a trick to pass goods through customs, pried open one of them with a crowbar and was nearly **blinded by a flash of blue glyphfire**. When he threw in a silver ring and slammed the lid, the second box—untouched and halfway across the room—clicked and opened. The ring was inside.
That was when Jackie knew: **this was worth more than any coin purse**. He named them _“Left Pocket” and “Right Pocket.”_
